    • Current Major Humanitarian Crises!

    Humanitarian Crises affect the lives of millions of people all around the world, these countries in particular have faced years of torment and have not received the awarenes that we believe they should have.

    Yemen

    Yemen is currently going through the largest humanitarian crisis in the world with over 80 percent of their population in need of humanitarian assistance. Additionally over 10.2 million children do not have access to health care. To learn more about the Yemen Crisis click this card

    Democratic Republic of Congo

    The Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) has gone well over 20 years without peace. Roughly 13 percent of people in the Congo are in desperate need of aid, along with this 1 in 10 children are predicted to die before the age of 5. To learn more about the DRC Crisis click this card

    Syria

    The Syrian Crisis initially started due to an uprise in violence which eventually led to a civil war. These outbreaks of violence has lead to well over 400,000 casualties along with millions of people forced out of their homes. To learn more about the Syrian Crisis click this card

    Nigeria

    The now 10 year old humanitarian crisis in Nigeria has lead to approximately 267,000 newborn babies dying every year (second most in the world) as well as over 13.2 million children without any form of education (the highest amount in the world) To learn more about the Nigerian Crisis click this card

    Venezuela

    The socioeconomic Crisis in Venezuela started in 2010 with Hugo Chávez as the country's president and has continued into the presidency of Nicolás Maduro. Their presidency has been widely considered the reason behind the increase in hyperinflation, escalating starvation disease, crime and mortality rates. To learn more about the Venezuela Crisis click this card

    Afghanistan

    The Crisis in Afghanistan initially started due to the increase of terrorist activity leading to nearly 100,000 deaths over the past 19 years.
